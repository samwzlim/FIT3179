All About WAGE$

Money plays an important role in our everyday lives, as it is used for our basic needs, from food to shelter, to healthcare and education.

In this data visualization, we will explore wage differences across the world and the factors that cause it, as well as the implications it has.

Minimum Wages Around the World

The purpose of a minimum wage is to establish a baseline income level for workers in return for their labor, which helps to prevent the exploitation of low-wage workers and ensures that they are able to afford basic necessities.

Minimum wages are different in every country due to a number of factors, such as the cost of living, inflation, currency differences, and many more.

In this choropleth map, the color saturation indicates the value of the hourly minimum wage. The more saturated the country, the higher the minimum wage.

* Use the slider to filter countries with wages above a minimum value.

Does GDP play a role in Minimum Wage differences?

Gross Domestic Product (or GDP for short), is an economic indicator that represents the total monetary value of all finished goods and services produced within a country's borders during a specific period.

This pannable and zoomable bubble plot allows us to directly compare the relationship between the 2 attributes, with the color indicating the continent and size of the bubbles indicating the population size.

* Select Continent to filter by continent and use the slider to filter countries with a population size larger than a minimum value.

How has minimum wage changed over time?

Minimum wage is likely to fluctuate over time, as a result of policy changes in response to economic and political reasons.

This area chart displays the changes in minimum wage per country. We can do a direct comparison of the changes in minimum wages between two countries at the same time.

* Select a country to view data for. The bottom area chart is an overview that allows for filtering of time period.

How many days does it take to afford the new iPhone 15 Pro?

Taking into account the local price of the iPhone 15 Pro (128GB) on average wages, countries are ranked from the most number of working days to the least number of working days required to earn wages equivalent to the price of an iPhone.

People earning average wages in Turkey require 123.7 days of work on average to be able to afford the latest iPhone.

We can see that in more developed countries such as the United States, Switzerland, etc., people only need to work for a fewer number of days to be able to afford the latest iPhone.

Hovering over a bar will give the price of the iPhone in local currency, and clicking on it will open the official website to purchase the phone.

* Use the minimap on the right to filter by section.
